PDF version

  1. If 0 is a regular value of the a smooth function \(f : \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}\), then prove that \(f^{-1}{0}\) is always an orientable manifold.

  2. Prove that every Lie group is orientable.

  3. Prove that if a manifold has trivial tangent bundle then it is orientable.

  4. Prove that the total space, \(T(M)\) of a tangent bundle, \(\pi : T(M) \to M\), of a manifold \(M\) is always orientable, even if \(M\) is not orientable.

  5. Prove that \(F : M \to N\) is a diffeomorphism between compact manifolds \(M\) and \(N\), and \(\omega\) a smooth \(n\)-form, where \(n\) is the dimension of \(N\), then \(\int_M F^*(\omega) = \pm \int_N \omega\)

  6. Compute \(H^k(\mathbb{R})\) for each \(k\).

  7. Prove that an exact form on a compact manifold cannot be no-where vanishing (Hint: maxima and minuma). Using this, what can you deduce about \(H^n(M)\) for an \(n\)-dimensional compact, connected, and orientable, manifold \(M\)?
