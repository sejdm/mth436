  1. Compute the knot group of the \(n\)-component link.

  2. Use the fundamental group to prove that the figure eight knot is non-trivial.

  3. Prove that infinite cyclic cover of the complement of a knot that we constructed in class is indeed a cover.

  4. Prove that the infinite cyclic cover of the knot complement is unique.

  5. For the complement of a knot \(X:=S^3\setminus K\), construct a finite cycle cover \(X_k\) with deck transformation group \(\mathbb{Z}/k\) and prove that it is indeed a covering space.

  6. Prove that the infinite cyclic cover covers \(X_k\) and that \(X_k\) is a quotient of the infinite cyclic cover.

  7. Prove that for each \(k \in \mathbb{N}\), there is a unique \(X_k\) covering \(S^3 \setminus K\).
